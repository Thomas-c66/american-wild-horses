Wild Horses

American Wild Horses: An Icon Of The Wild is an educational website focusing on why wild horses need our help to continue being free and their survival. Wild horses have been an Americasn icon since the mid 1500's when Spanish arrived in North America. Several horses esacped captivity and so bgan the American Mustangs journey. According to the American wild horse campain, in the 19th century there were an estimated to be around two million wild horses. By 1971 there were only around 17,000. More than 1 million horses were used in World War 1 for combat, the rest were hunted for their skin, for chicken feed, dog food, and just for the sport. They were chased by helicopters, run down by motorized vehicles until exhausted, ran off cliffs, and shot in corralled bloodbaths. After witnessing how wild horses were being pipelined out of the West Velma Johnston a.k.a Wildhorse Annie, began a crusade involving school children and their mothers that led to the 1959 law that prohibited the use of helicopter and motorized to capture wild horses.

In 1971 the letters continued in fact there more letters than any other non-war issues than anytime in the history of the United States. With one congressman alone recieving more than 14,000 letters. On December 15, 1971 President Richard Nixon signed into law the Wild Free-Roaming Horse & Burro Act (you can read the law by clicking the button below). The Bureau of Land Management (BLM) and the U.S. Forest Service were appointed to implement the 1971 Act. Their responsibilities also include issuing public land grazing permits to cattle ranchers. Wild Free-Roaming horse and Burro Act

In 1959 a woman known as Wild Horse Annie learned of the ruthless killing of wild horses and led a campaign involving school children, and newspapers printed articels about the exploitation of horses and burro's. In 1959 the Wild Horse Annie Act prohibited the use of motorized vehicles to hunt wild horses and burrors on public land but did little to protect them. The Wild Free-Roming Horse and Burrow act was signed into law in 1971 by Richard Nixon. The law was placed to protect, manage, and control the Horses and Burro's on public land. The Blm was asssigned to manage the horses. I have included a link in the about section to the details of the law.

BLM Roundups

The BLM assigned to protect wild horses have become their worst nightmare. The Blm was assigned to mange and protect the wild horses instead are out to remove them from the public lands. The BLM leases thousands of acres of public land to ranchers who graze their cattle. Because the ranchers are complaining the horses are over-grazing the public land the BLM has falsified records in order to remove the horses. Today the BLM removes thousands of wild horses through roundups that they claim are humane.
